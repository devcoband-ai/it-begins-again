# The Floor Remembers

**Artist:** Jeff (through female persona)
**Status:** Draft — written for Chapter 7
**Context:** The narrator hears Noah's guitar for the first time in thirty-two years and writes over it. Not David's style — Jeff's. Systems thinker. Direct. The metaphor earns its place or it doesn't stay.

---

[Verse 1]
I heard you through a file
Sent at an hour no one should be awake
Six minutes of guitar and no explanation
The way you always were
Nothing announced
Just the thing itself

[Verse 2]
Thirty-two years between the last time I stood in your room
And the moment I pressed play on a Tuesday morning
In a kitchen that looks nothing like Sandersville
You don't know I'm listening
You don't know I wrote this

[Chorus]
Take me with you
Where you go
I don't need to understand it
I just need to feel the floor

[Verse 3]
I built systems for a living
Traced requirements to their source
Governed artifacts through pipelines
Named every dependency and force
And none of it prepared me
For what happened when I heard your guitar
And the words came out
Like I was still the bassist
Trying to find the pocket
In somebody else's song

[Bridge]
David drives through the night to get to you
Past bridges he looked at too long
Past storage units and the last of his credit
And the only plan he has
Is Friday

[Chorus]
Take me with you
Where you go
I don't need to understand it
I just need to feel the floor

[Verse 4]
The wheels on this car
Are going to take us somewhere
I don't know where
I didn't build the route
I didn't draw the map
I just heard the frequency
And I shaped within it
The way I always did
Before I learned to call it architecture

[Outro]
You had cheap gear in 1994
And something underneath the sound
That I couldn't name then
And can't name now
But the floor remembers
Even when the room doesn't
Even when the people don't
The floor
Remembers
