# Literary DNA

The narrative influences. None are cited in the text. All shape the prose.

---

## The Town

### Tom Wolfe — *A Man in Full*
- **What it gives us:** Marietta. The New South. Atlanta sprawl. Generic suburban Georgia — strip malls on red clay, a place that could be anywhere. The South paved over.
- **How it enters the prose:** The Marietta sections should feel flat, manufactured, interchangeable. No texture. No weight. The contrast with Milledgeville is the point.

### John Berendt — *Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil*
- **What it gives us:** Arriving in a Georgia town as an outsider. Old money, older secrets. Eccentrics with real power. Beauty draped over darkness. Rules nobody explains.
- **How it enters the prose:** The Milledgeville arrival. The sense of a town with a surface and a depth. Spanish moss and good manners and something underneath. DoDo as a Midnight character.

### Flannery O'Connor
- **What it gives us:** The grotesque inside the ordinary. Misfits and grace. Violence underneath manners. Peacocks on a lawn. She went to Jeff's college. She died at Andalusia, outside Milledgeville, in 1964.
- **How it enters the prose:** Roger, DoDo, the old men, the pool hall itself. Ordinary people in an ordinary place, and something underneath you're not ready for. Referenced obliquely in Ch. 5 ("a farm just outside of town where a woman had raised peacocks") — never named.

## The Voice

### Bob Dylan
- **What he gives us:** Plain language hiding depth. Observational. Selection over explanation. You don't tell the reader why it matters. You put them there.
- **How it enters the prose:** Chapter 5's tone. The pool hall chapter. No frameworks, no cosmic architecture. Just a place and the people in it.

### Mike Watt (Minutemen, fIREHOSE)
- **What he gives us:** Working-class storytelling. "We jam econo." DIY, anti-pretension. The van, the gear, the grind. Brilliant people who don't perform brilliance.
- **How it enters the prose:** Roger. The old snooker players. The dollar hot dog. The refusal to dress things up.

## The Structure

### The DevCo Platform (Chapter 6)
- **What it gives us:** Infrastructure as narrator. Metadata as voice. The machine that stores the artifacts doesn't have opinions about them.
- **How it enters the prose:** Chapter 6 only. Record IDs, timestamps, clinical observations. The one chapter where a human emotion leaks through ("It sounds exactly right") is immediately flagged as out of scope.

---

## The Counterweight

The book's tension lives between two poles:

**David's pole:** Apollinaire, cummings, Norelli-Bachelet, sacred geometry, Human Design. Complexity as truth. Form as condensed time. The cosmic.

**Jeff's pole:** Dylan, Watt, O'Connor, Berendt. Simplicity as truth. A dollar hot dog. The grounded.

Ancient Country Wisdom needs both. The book enacts the same tension — chapters of framework (2, 3, 4) followed by chapters of ground (5) followed by the machine speaking for itself (6).
