# Threads

Recurring motifs, images, and ideas that connect across chapters.

---

## The Lottery Thread
Roger → DoDo → the Texaco. Three ways to approach the same impossible thing.

- **1989:** Roger, 22, math/CS student behind the bar at DoDo's, trying to beat the Georgia Lottery with algorithms. Before data science was a word.
- **~2003–2008:** DoDo (James E. Hollis) wins the Georgia Lottery. No public record found, but local knowledge. The boss won the thing the mathematician was trying to solve. Without the math.
- **2025:** A Hancock County man wins $1M from a VIP Billionaire scratch-off at the Texaco on N. Columbia St in Milledgeville. The town keeps playing.

The mathematician, the boss, and the daily players — same town, same dream, decades apart.

_Not for Chapter 5. Pays off later._

## The Architecture Thread
Antebellum gothic. The weight of buildings that outlasted the people who built them.

- **Marietta:** No architecture. No history you could feel. The New South.
- **Milledgeville:** Columns, porticos, the Old Governor's Mansion. Sherman didn't burn it. The buildings survived. The accumulation of layers.
- **The pool hall:** Old in the right way. Old because nobody bothered to make it new.
- **Jeff and Greg:** Shared appreciation for this architecture. The tension between beauty and what it was built on.
- **O'Connor:** "A farm just outside of town where a woman had raised peacocks." The gothic is the ordinary with something underneath.

## The Consolidation Thread
Things built by many hands, concentrated into few.

- **1989:** DoDo owns the pool hall and the Opera House. Two rooms, one man.
- **1990–94:** Jeff's generation builds the strip. Multiple owners. The scene distributes.
- **2017:** Frank Pendergast buys DoDo's buildings. Already owns The Brick. Three of five venues, one owner.
- **Things change names and keep their addresses. Or they keep their names and change what they mean.**

## The Silence Thread
People who don't respond. Spaces between notes.

- **Greg:** Center of gravity. Hasn't responded to reunion. Quiet — but Greg was always quiet.
- **Noah:** Quiet the way Noah was always quiet.
- **The pool hall:** Silence as atmosphere. No jukebox. No music. Just snooker balls and Roger's voice.
- **Chapter 6:** "No response was logged." The platform doesn't distinguish between silence as refusal and silence as consideration.
- **David's conducting:** He would wait for the music to earn his entrance. Silence as direction.

## The Starting Over Thread
Arriving somewhere with nothing.

- **Jeff at 19:** The lost year. Starting over wasn't a choice. Knew nobody.
- **The pool hall:** A place that doesn't ask you to be anyone. Sitting is enough.
- **The song:** Making music again after twenty years. No band. No rehearsal. Just a prompt.
- **The book itself:** "It begins, again."

## The Infrastructure Thread
People who build the rooms versus people who fill them.

- **DoDo:** Built/owned the rooms. Never performed. Controlled the infrastructure of culture.
- **Scott Cameron, Mitch Brooks:** Built the strip. Created the stages others would play on.
- **Roger:** Maintained the space. Made conversation. Served the hot dogs.
- **The DevCo Platform:** Stores the artifacts. Doesn't interpret them.
- **Greg:** Held the center of gravity. Opened the low end instead of crowding it. Made room.
