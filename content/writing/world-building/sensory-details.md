# Sensory Details

What the places look, sound, smell, taste, and feel like. Memory as material.

---

## DoDo's Pool Hall — Daytime, Summer 1989

### Sight
- Wood floors, dark and scarred, creaking since Eisenhower
- Brick walls
- Professional snooker tables — full-size, green felt stretched tight, clean rails
- Hanging brass lamps with green glass shades over each table
- Daylight leaking through front windows but mostly the room makes its own atmosphere
- Cave-dark beyond the lamp pools
- Antique junk on the walls — old signs, a clock that may not work
- Hot dog warmer on the bar counter — rotating metal rollers
- Big windows with arching glass (original façade)
- No neon. No fluorescents.
- All men. Shirtsleeves. Focused.

### Sound
- Crack of snooker balls — quieter than pool, more precise
- Roger's voice from behind the counter
- Maybe a radio. Maybe nothing.
- The deliberate movement of men around tables
- No jukebox. No music.
- The creak of wood floors when someone steps wrong

### Smell
- Hot dogs on the warmer
- Mustard
- Cigarette smoke (you could still smoke inside in 1989)
- Old wood — decades of absorption
- Old brick absorbing summers

### Touch
- Georgia summer heat outside — hundred degrees, humidity like breathing through a wet towel
- Air conditioning inside (the reason to walk in?)
- The felt of the table under your hand
- The stool at the counter

### Taste
- Dollar hot dogs

## Milledgeville — Summer 1989, General

### Sight
- Antebellum columns on houses too beautiful for a town that size
- The Old Governor's Mansion
- Brick facades downtown, standing since before the Civil War
- Vacant lots next to old houses with wraparound porches
- A main street that could be a movie set
- The asphalt shimmering at two in the afternoon
- A skeleton town in summer — half the students gone

### Feel
- Heavier than Marietta. Older.
- The weight of a place that carries its history in its walls
- A town that knew something you didn't and wasn't in a hurry to tell you
- Southern hospitality that's real but doesn't need you
- The compression — two colleges, one strip, everybody funnels through

## Marietta, GA — Late 1980s

### Sight
- Strip malls and subdivisions
- Highways that all look the same
- New construction on red clay
- Fast food chains replacing whatever was there before
- A mall

### Feel
- Generic. Could be anywhere in the Sun Belt.
- No architecture. No history you could feel.
- Boredom safely contained by the presence of a mall.
