- **From:** Marietta, GA (Cobb County)
- **Arrived Milledgeville:** Summer 1989, age 19 (a year late — something happened between 18-19)
- **Instruments:** Bass
- **Role in ACW:** Bassist, fifth member to join
- **Chapter appearances:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 (first person), 6 (third person via platform)
- **Present day:** 55, CTO, writing this book
- **Key detail:** Shared appreciation for antebellum gothic architecture with Greg

## David Perry
- **Band:** Hero (before ACW), then Ancient Country Wisdom — singer
- **Voice:** Conducted the band with his vocals. Waited for the music to earn his entrance.
- **Frameworks:** Cloud seeding, Human Design, the spectrum, Apollinaire, e.e. cummings
- **Chapter appearances:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 (referenced)
- **Present day:** In active dialogue with Jeff. Talking about spectrums and coherence.
- **Key detail:** Creates the way other people breathe — continuously, without announcing it

## Rich
- **Role in ACW:** Guitar, the connector — introduced Jeff to David
- **Voice:** Shy troubadour. More moxie than he let himself reveal.
- **Instrument:** Rickenbacker
- **Chapter appearances:** 1
- **Present day:** Status unclear. "Somewhere."
- **Key detail:** Talked about Bob Dylan as a thinker who happened to write music

## Greg
- **Role in ACW:** Drummer. The center of gravity.
- **From:** Sandersville, GA (where the band rehearsed)
- **Style:** Precision of a jazz musician. Compact kit. Every hit meant something. Nothing wasted.
- **Chapter appearances:** 1, 5 (present-day section), 6 (referenced)
- **Present day:** Has not responded to reunion. Quiet — but Greg was always quiet.
- **Key detail:** Shared Jeff's appreciation for antebellum gothic architecture. "Embodied Sandersville itself — mystical, distant, unhurried."

## Noah
- **Role in ACW:** Lead guitar
- **Chapter appearances:** 1, 6 (referenced)

## Roger — The Oracle
- **Age in 1989:** ~22
- **Occupation:** Math and computer science student; worked the bar at DoDo's daytime
- **Chapter appearances:** 5
- **Key detail:** Trying to beat the Georgia Lottery with algorithms. Smarter than the bar he was standing behind. Served hot dogs for a dollar.
- **Present day:** Unknown. Lost touch.

- **Chapter appearances:** 5 (referenced), 6 (referenced via platform)
- **Key detail:** Won the Georgia Lottery (~2003-2008). Died ~2016. Buildings sold to Frank Pendergast.
- **Business partner:** Danny Snow
