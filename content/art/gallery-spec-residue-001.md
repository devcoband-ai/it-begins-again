# Gallery Spec: Residue-001 — Pure Joy Sound

## Specification

---

### Classification

**Gallery Persona:** The Residue, bleeding into The Seismograph at the edges
**Frequency Coordinates:**
- Atmospheric ↔ Dense: **atmospheric** — this piece breathes. Space is the material.
- Natural ↔ Urban: **natural** — wood, string, skin, air. Nothing manufactured.
- Suggestion ↔ Brutalist: **deep suggestion** — the lightest touch in the gallery. Almost not there.

---

### The Song as Brief

Pure Joy Sound. David and Noah, probably 1998. Just a one off improv. Two acoustic instruments in a room, no plan, no structure, just shape. Percussive guitar with hammer-ons. A lyric about sobbing and laughing occupying the same gesture. "Four points in space." A color that is a sound. Joy as practice, not arrival.

David kept this recording for twenty-eight years.

The piece is about what twenty-eight years does to something small that someone kept.

---

### Composition

**Perspective:** Intimate. Close-up. The viewer is six inches from a surface. Not surveilling, not submerged — pressing their face against something to read what time wrote on it.

**Focal structure:** A single surface — wood. Not a guitar. Not an instrument. Just wood grain. The kind of close-up where the material could be a tabletop, a floor, a door, a soundboard. The ambiguity is structural. The viewer who knows the song sees a guitar body. The viewer who doesn't sees a surface that has been touched for a very long time.

**The grain:** Wood grain rendered at macro scale — the annual rings, the medullary rays, the patterns that are literally the record of the tree's years. Each ring is a year. The grain is a timeline. Twenty-eight rings would be visible if you counted, but the piece doesn't ask you to count. It asks you to notice that time is stored in material.

**The wear:** This is the Residue. Across the wood grain, evidence of contact:
- A pale oval where a forearm rested — the finish worn through to bare wood by years of playing position
- Fine scratches in parallel — pick marks, or fingernails, or just the accumulation of hands moving across the surface
- A faint darkening along one edge — oil from skin, absorbed into the grain over decades
- One deeper mark — a gouge, a dent, a moment of force that the surface recorded and never released

**The ghost:** At the upper portion of the frame, barely visible, a warmth — not a figure, not a shape, just a thermal presence. A faint amber glow in the wood as if the surface is still warm from the last hand that touched it. The residue of contact. Not the person. The heat they left.

**Surrounding field:** The wood grain extends to all edges but transitions at the periphery. Lower-left: the grain lines begin to loosen, soften, curve — becoming topographic contour lines (the Seismograph bleeding in). The wood becomes terrain. The guitar becomes geology. The intimate becomes vast without a cut or a border. The transition is gradual — you can't point to where wood becomes earth.

---

### Color Specification

**Dominant palette:** Warm wood tones only. This is the most restricted palette in the gallery.

- Deep grain: dark walnut (#3E2723 to #5D4037)
- Light grain: honey amber (#C19A6B to #DEB887)
- Wear marks: pale bone (#E8D8C4 to #F5F0E8) — the bare wood exposed beneath finish
- Oil darkening: deeper than surrounding grain (#2E1F14) — concentrated at edges
- Ghost warmth: faint amber (#FFCC66 at 8–12% opacity) — barely perceptible, more felt than seen
- Seismograph bleed (periphery): the wood tones shift cooler — amber becomes ochre, walnut becomes slate, the grain lines take on the blue-green tint of geological survey (#6B8E7B to #4A6670)

**No blue. No neon. No circuit amber.** This piece exists before electricity. The Switchboard hasn't arrived yet. The only light is organic — the glow of something alive that was recently held.

---

### Line Language

**One grammar only: growth.**

All lines in this piece are lines of growth. Wood grain. Annual rings. The slow calligraphy of a tree recording its own life. No right angles. No routing. No engineering. The Switchboard is absent. The Seismograph enters only as the wood grain loosens into terrain at the edges — but even those contour lines are organic, following the logic of growth and pressure, not measurement.

**The wear marks break the grain.** The scratches and the gouge are the only lines that don't follow the wood's natural pattern. They're imposed — the record of human contact cutting across the record of the tree's life. Two timelines on one surface: the tree's and the player's. Neither is labeled. Both are legible.

---

### Texture

This is the most textural piece in the gallery. The other pieces are luminous — they emit light, glow, project. This piece absorbs.

**Surface quality:** Matte. The finish is mostly worn through. What remains is the wood itself — dry, warm, tactile. The viewer should feel they could reach out and touch it. The grain has tooth. The wear is smooth. The contrast between the rough and the smooth is the haptic story.

**Depth:** Almost flat. This isn't a deep image — it's a shallow one. The surface is everything. What depth exists comes from the grain itself — the way darker rings recede slightly and lighter rings advance. And from the Seismograph bleed at the edges, where the flattening surface begins to suggest depth, curvature, earth.

**No glow. No bloom. No particles.** This piece is the opposite of the Threshold hand. Where that piece was translucent and luminous, this piece is opaque and absorptive. Light lands here and stays.

### Song Seeds

Pure Joy Sound. Only Pure Joy Sound.

This piece does not multi-seed. The other specs draw from two or three songs. This one draws from one — because the song itself was one. A one-off improv. Two people. One room. No plan. The piece inherits that singularity. It doesn't reference the network or the infrastructure or the geometry. It references the fact that someone played something once and someone else kept it for twenty-eight years, and the keeping is the art.

"I'm not talking about flying away. No baby. I'm talking about four points in space."

Four points in space. The four edges of the frame. The wood inside them. The time stored in the grain. The warmth still in the surface.

---

### Gallery Title

*Probably '98*

Three syllables from David's caption when he sent the file. "Me and Noah probably 98. Just a one off improv." The title tells a stranger nothing. It tells a reader everything — the year, the minimizing, the twenty-eight years of keeping, the casual tossing off of something that lives in the center of your chest.

---

### Placement Notes

This piece hangs early in the gallery sequence. Before the Switchboard. Before the Threshold. Before the density arrives. The viewer encounters wood and warmth and silence before they encounter circuits and collisions. The gallery starts where the music started — two people in a room, one surface, no infrastructure. Everything that follows is what happened after they stopped playing and the world kept going.
