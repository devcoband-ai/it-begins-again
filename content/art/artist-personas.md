# Artist Personas

Detailed personas that define artistic styles and approaches for AI-generated artwork in *It Begins, Again*.

---

## The Archivist

**Style:** Documentary realism with warmth. Analog texture — film grain, natural light, muted palette. Captures places and objects as they were, not as nostalgia wants them to be.

**Use for:** Chapter headers set in the past (Milledgeville, Sandersville, the pool hall, the strip). Photographs of real places reimagined. Anything grounded in geography and memory.

**References:** William Eggleston's color photography. Sally Mann's Southern landscapes. The grain of a disposable camera from 1993.

**Tone:** Honest. Unhurried. The camera doesn't judge. It records.

---

## The Conductor

**Style:** Abstract, kinetic, layered. Sacred geometry rendered as visual music — interference patterns, wave forms, pentagonal and hexagonal structures. Deep color fields with light breaking through. Movement implied, never frozen.

**References:** Hilma af Klint's cosmological paintings. Bridget Riley's optical work. Cymatics photography. The visual language of sound made visible.

**Use for:** Conceptual pieces — the geometry of the band, the bodygraph, the pattern resynchronizing. Art that accompanies the cosmic frame, not the human story.

**Tone:** Precise but alive. The geometry breathes.

---

## The Drifter

**Style:** Impressionistic, hazy, liminal. Soft focus. Light bleeding at the edges. The world seen through heat shimmer or the first moment after waking. Colors lean warm — amber, ochre, faded denim blue.

**References:** Gerhard Richter's photo-paintings. Terrence Malick's cinematography. The light in a Georgia summer at 4pm.

**Use for:** Emotional beats — drifting, isolation, the gap years, the moment before recognition. Art that feels like memory, not documentation.

**Tone:** Tender. Uncertain. Beautiful without insisting on it.

---

## The Transmitter

**Style:** High contrast, graphic, electric. Clean lines over dark fields. Neon or phosphor glow against deep black. The visual equivalent of a signal cutting through noise — rare radio made visible.

**References:** Saul Bass title sequences. Blue Note album covers. The glow of a tube amp in a dark room.

**Use for:** Music-facing artwork — album art, song visualizations, anything tied to the recordings and seeds. Art for the collabs and covers.

**Tone:** Direct. Confident. The signal is clear.
