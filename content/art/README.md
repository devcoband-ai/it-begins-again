## The Gallery Art

A parallel visual world seeded by the music, not the memoir. The gallery looks nothing like the book. The book tells the story. The gallery lets the songs tell a different one — oblique, metaphysical, felt before understood.

The underlying mathematics is the Mandelbrot set — bounded iteration, self-similarity across scale, infinite complexity at the boundary. The band as a function that never diverged. See [gallery-personas.md](gallery-personas.md) for the full framework.

### Gallery Personas

Four personas, each a frequency range rather than a fixed style:

| Persona | Frequency Range | What It Renders |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **The Seismograph** | Atmospheric → mid-dense, natural → liminal | Sub-audible pressure as terrain. The floor frequency made visible. |
| **The Residue** | Atmospheric, natural → abandoned-urban | Aftermath as material. What thirty years leaves on a surface. |
| **The Diffusion Chamber** | Dense, urban → post-urban | Coherence prevented. The Mandelbrot boundary — maximum complexity before escape. |
| **The Threshold** | Full spectrum — exists only at transitions | The seam between any two personas. Diptych. Collision. The boundary itself. |

## The Two Worlds

The book art is documentary. Warm. Grounded in Milledgeville and Sandersville and the rooms where things happened. It serves the reader.

The gallery art is metaphysical. It starts from the songs and moves toward abstraction — terrain, texture, diffusion, sacred geometry, fractal mathematics. It serves the viewer who may never read the book.

The book art says: *this is where it happened.*
The gallery art says: *this is what it felt like.*

They never cross. The crosswalk is this README.
