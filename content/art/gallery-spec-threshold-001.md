# Gallery Spec: Threshold-001

## Reverse-Engineered Specification

---

### Classification

**Gallery Persona:** The Threshold (Seismograph ↔ Switchboard collision)
**Frequency Coordinates:**
- Atmospheric ↔ Dense: **dense** — every square inch carries information, no negative space
- Natural ↔ Urban: **the seam** — natural and urban coexist in the same plane, neither dominant
- Suggestion ↔ Brutalist: **mid-suggestion** — the hand is direct but the context is layered enough to remain ambiguous

---

### Composition

**Perspective:** Slightly below eye-level, looking upward at approximately 15–20° from horizontal. The viewer is underneath the surface, looking up through it. The hand rises from below the viewer's plane into the space above. This is not surveillance (Switchboard-001's angle). This is submersion.

**Focal structure:** A translucent human hand, open-palmed, fingers spread, rising vertically through the center of the frame. The hand is constructed from point-cloud particles — thousands of tiny luminous dots forming the shape without solid surfaces. The hand is recognizably human but not flesh. It is data. It is frequency. It is crystalline structure made visible.

**The collision plane:** The hand passes through a horizontal surface where two visual systems meet:

- **Below/behind the hand (Seismograph territory):** Topographic contour lines — organic, flowing, concentric wave patterns. The lines follow terrain logic — ridges, valleys, pressure gradients. Colors shift warm-to-cool across elevation: deep crimson and burgundy at the lowest contours, through amber and yellow-green at mid-elevation, into blue and violet at the peaks. The contours ripple outward from the hand as if the hand is the source of the seismic event.

- **Above/around the hand (Switchboard territory):** Circuit traces — rectilinear routing paths, junction nodes (concentric amber circles), hexagonal enclosures. White and amber lines on the darker field. PCB conventions — right angles, 45° diagonals, directional flow. The Switchboard network from the first piece is present but partially submerged under the contour waves.

**The seam:** There is no clean division. The two systems interpenetrate. Contour lines flow through circuit traces. Junction nodes sit at the intersections of topographic ridges. The hexagonal structures emerge from the contour wave patterns as if the terrain itself is crystallizing into infrastructure. The hand is the site of maximum interpenetration — both systems converge on it.

**Edge behavior:** Dense at all edges. The contour waves and circuit traces both extend beyond the frame. The composition is a section cut from an infinite field, same as Switchboard-001, but here the field is layered — geology underneath, electronics on top, the hand passing through both.

---

### Color Specification

**Background field:** Dark — not the clean navy of Switchboard-001. Darker, muddier, approximately #0D1117 to #1A1A2E. The darkness of deep ground, not deep screen. Brown-black undertones where the Seismograph dominates, blue-black where the Switchboard takes over.

**Contour lines (Seismograph layer):**
- Deep elevation: crimson to burgundy (#8B1A1A to #4A0E0E)
- Mid elevation: amber to yellow-green (#C4883A to #7AB648)
- High elevation: cyan to blue (#3AAFCF to #2255AA)
- Peak elevation: violet (#7744BB to #AA55DD)
- Line weight: 1–2px, consistent. Lines are smooth, organic curves. No right angles.

**Circuit traces (Switchboard layer):**
- Primary traces: white to pale blue-white (#CCDDEE to #FFFFFF)
- Junction nodes: warm amber cores (#D4A44A) with concentric rings
- Hexagonal enclosures: white outline, lower opacity than primary traces (~40%)
- Line weight: 1–2px, angular. Right angles and 45° diagonals.

**Hand (point cloud):**
- Particle color: pale cyan-white (#B8E8F0 to #FFFFFF)
- Particle density: highest at fingertips and palm center, sparser at wrist/edges — the hand is more resolved where it's most human
- Particle glow: soft bloom, cool-tinted (#88CCDD at ~30% opacity)
- Transparency: the hand is translucent — both the contour lines and circuit traces are visible through it. The hand doesn't obscure. It mediates.

**Atmospheric bleed:** Where the Seismograph and Switchboard layers overlap, the colors mix — amber junction nodes sitting in violet contour fields produce a warm haze. Green contour lines crossing white circuit traces produce a cool shimmer. The color interactions are additive, not painted. Light mixing, not pigment mixing.

---

### Line Language

**Two competing grammars in one frame:**

1. **Organic (Seismograph):** Smooth curves, concentric waves, no sharp angles. The lines flow like water or sound waves in sand. They respond to the hand — rippling outward from it as if disturbed by its presence.

2. **Engineered (Switchboard):** Right angles, 45° routing, junction nodes, directional markers. The lines ignore the hand — they run through it, over it, under it, indifferent. The infrastructure doesn't care about the body. The body disrupts the terrain but not the circuit.

**The tension:** Organic lines respond to the hand. Engineered lines don't. This is the visual argument of the piece. The natural world registers the body. The infrastructure doesn't. Both are present. Neither wins.

---

### The Hand

**Construction:** Point cloud / particle mesh. Not wireframe (too mechanical), not solid render (too literal). The particles suggest form without confirming it. At a distance, you see a hand. Up close, you see a field of luminous dots that could be anything — stars, data points, cells, atoms.

**Gesture:** Open palm, fingers spread, rising. Not grasping. Not reaching. Presenting. Or receiving. The gesture is ambiguous — is the hand offering something upward or catching something falling? The piece doesn't answer.

**Position:** Center-frame, slightly left of geometric center. The hand rises from the lower third into the upper third. The wrist disappears below the contour surface. The hand is emerging from the ground — from the Seismograph's terrain — into the Switchboard's circuit space.

**Five fingers:** Five points. Not incidental. The hand is a pentagon. The fingers are nodes. The palm is the empty center. The body *is* the geometry, rendered as anatomy instead of schematic.

---

### Depth and Atmosphere

**Depth of field:** Moderate. The hand is sharpest at the fingertips and softens toward the wrist where it submerges. The Switchboard traces in the upper-right are crisper than the Seismograph contours in the lower-left. Focus follows the hand's trajectory — sharp where it's arriving, soft where it came from.

**Atmospheric density:** High. The space between layers is not empty — it's filled with luminous haze where the two systems bleed into each other. The atmosphere is not fog. It's interference. Two signal systems occupying the same frequency band and producing visible artifacts where they overlap.

**Layering:** Four visual layers:
1. **Seismograph substrate** — contour terrain (deepest, warmest)
2. **Switchboard network** — circuit traces (mid-depth, cooler)
3. **Interference haze** — where layers 1 and 2 overlap (atmospheric, additive color)
4. **The hand** — point cloud (foreground, coolest, highest luminance)

### Song Seeds

This piece lives at the threshold between:
- **Rare Radio** (Seismograph) — the floor frequency, the terrain that responds to the body
- **The Ballad of Ancient Country Wisdom** (Switchboard) — the full network, indifferent to any single node

The hand is the collab moment. The point where the body enters the infrastructure. The point where someone shapes within a structure they didn't build.

The five fingers are the five members. The open palm is the empty center. The hand rising through the terrain into the circuit space is the band forming — the body passing through the natural into the engineered, carrying both, belonging to neither.
