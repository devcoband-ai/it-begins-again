# Gallery Spec: Threshold-001

## Reverse-Engineered Specification

---

### Classification

**Gallery Persona:** The Threshold (Seismograph ↔ Diffusion Chamber collision)
**Frequency Coordinates:**
- Atmospheric ↔ Dense: **dense** — every square inch carries information, no negative space
- Natural ↔ Urban: **the seam** — the organic and the metaphysical coexist in the same plane, neither dominant
- Suggestion ↔ Brutalist: **mid-suggestion** — the hand is direct but the context is layered enough to remain ambiguous

---

### Composition

**Perspective:** Slightly below eye-level, looking upward at approximately 15–20° from horizontal. The viewer is underneath the surface, looking up through it. The hand rises from below the viewer's plane into the space above. This is submersion. The viewer is inside the terrain, looking up at something emerging.

**Focal structure:** A translucent human hand, open-palmed, fingers spread, rising vertically through the center of the frame. The hand is constructed from point-cloud particles — thousands of tiny luminous dots forming the shape without solid surfaces. The hand is recognizably human but not flesh. It is frequency. It is crystalline structure made visible. The body translating light into form.

**The collision plane:** The hand passes through a horizontal surface where two visual systems meet:

- **Below/behind the hand (Seismograph territory):** Topographic contour lines — organic, flowing, concentric wave patterns. The lines follow terrain logic — ridges, valleys, pressure gradients. Colors shift warm-to-cool across elevation: deep crimson and burgundy at the lowest contours, through amber and yellow-green at mid-elevation, into blue and violet at the peaks. The contours ripple outward from the hand as if the hand is the source of the seismic event.

- **Above/around the hand (the meridian field):** Lines of force — not circuits, not wiring, but channels. The visual language of the bodygraph, of acupuncture meridians, of ley lines mapped across sacred landscape. White and amber pathways that follow an invisible geometry — connecting nodes that glow like chakra points or standing stones. The lines are straight where they carry force, curved where they carry reception. They form pentagons and hexagons not because they're engineered but because sacred geometry produces those shapes when forces align. The channels pass through the hand without responding to it — the geometry was here before the body arrived.

**The seam:** There is no clean division. The two systems interpenetrate. Contour lines flow through meridian channels. Energy nodes sit at the intersections of topographic ridges as if the landscape itself produced them — standing stones at the convergence of ley lines, acupuncture points on the body of the earth. The hand is the site of maximum interpenetration — both systems converge on it.

**Edge behavior:** Dense at all edges. The contour waves and meridian channels both extend beyond the frame. The composition is a section cut from an infinite field. The viewer is looking at a fragment of something vast — geology underneath, sacred geometry on top, the hand passing through both.

---

### Color Specification

**Background field:** Dark — deep ground, not deep screen. Approximately #0D1117 to #1A1A2E. Brown-black undertones where the Seismograph dominates, blue-black where the meridian field takes over.

**Contour lines (Seismograph layer):**
- Deep elevation: crimson to burgundy (#8B1A1A to #4A0E0E)
- Mid elevation: amber to yellow-green (#C4883A to #7AB648)
- High elevation: cyan to blue (#3AAFCF to #2255AA)
- Peak elevation: violet (#7744BB to #AA55DD)
- Line weight: 1–2px, consistent. Lines are smooth, organic curves. No right angles.

**Meridian channels (force layer):**
- Primary channels: white to pale blue-white (#CCDDEE to #FFFFFF)
- Energy nodes: warm amber cores (#D4A44A) with concentric rings — the visual language of defined centers, of standing stones seen from above, of points where force concentrates
- Hexagonal and pentagonal formations: white outline, lower opacity (~40%) — the geometry that emerges when channels of force intersect at sacred intervals
- Channel weight: 1–2px. Lines are deliberate — they carry intention. Some straight, some gently angular. Not the right angles of engineering. The angles of crystalline growth.

**Hand (point cloud):**
- Particle color: pale cyan-white (#B8E8F0 to #FFFFFF)
- Particle density: highest at fingertips and palm center, sparser at wrist/edges — the hand is more resolved where it's most human
- Particle glow: soft bloom, cool-tinted (#88CCDD at ~30% opacity)
- Transparency: the hand is translucent — both the contour lines and meridian channels are visible through it. The hand doesn't obscure. It mediates. It is the crystalline gate.

**Atmospheric bleed:** Where the Seismograph and meridian layers overlap, the colors mix — amber energy nodes sitting in violet contour fields produce a warm haze. Green contour lines crossing white channels produce a cool shimmer. The color interactions are additive — light mixing, not pigment mixing. The interference is visible. Two frequency systems occupying the same space.

### Line Language

**Two competing grammars in one frame:**

1. **Organic (Seismograph):** Smooth curves, concentric waves, no sharp angles. The lines flow like water or sound waves in sand. They respond to the hand — rippling outward from it as if disturbed by its presence. The earth feels the body.

2. **Sacred (the meridian field):** Deliberate pathways with the angles of crystal formation and sacred proportion. The lines carry force between nodes. They pass through the hand without responding to it — the geometry was here before the body arrived and will remain after it leaves. The sacred doesn't accommodate the individual. The individual learns to align with it.

**The tension:** Organic lines respond to the hand. Sacred lines don't. This is the visual argument of the piece. The natural world registers the body. The geometry doesn't. Both are present. Neither wins. The body is the mediator between terrain and force — translating one into the other, belonging to neither.

---

### The Hand

**Construction:** Point cloud / particle mesh. Not wireframe (too mechanical), not solid render (too literal). The particles suggest form without confirming it. At a distance, you see a hand. Up close, you see a field of luminous dots that could be anything — stars, cells, atoms, condensed light.

**Gesture:** Open palm, fingers spread, rising. Not grasping. Not reaching. Receiving. Or transmitting. The gesture is the ambiguity of the crystalline gate — is the hand processing incoming light or emitting it? The nervous system deciding: beneficial or overwhelming. The piece doesn't answer.

**Position:** Center-frame, slightly left of geometric center. The hand rises from the lower third into the upper third. The wrist disappears below the contour surface. The hand is emerging from the earth — from the Seismograph's terrain — into the meridian field's sacred space.

**Five fingers:** Five points. Not incidental. The hand is a pentagon. The fingers are nodes. The palm is the empty center. The body *is* the geometry, rendered as anatomy instead of abstraction.

---

### Depth and Atmosphere

**Depth of field:** Moderate. The hand is sharpest at the fingertips and softens toward the wrist where it submerges. The meridian channels in the upper-right are crisper than the Seismograph contours in the lower-left. Focus follows the hand's trajectory — sharp where it's arriving, soft where it came from.

**Atmospheric density:** High. The space between layers is not empty — it's filled with luminous haze where the two systems bleed into each other. The atmosphere is not fog. It's interference. Two frequency systems occupying the same space and producing visible artifacts where they overlap. The haze is what the body feels before the mind names it.

**Layering:** Four visual layers:
1. **Seismograph substrate** — contour terrain (deepest, warmest)
2. **Meridian field** — sacred geometry channels (mid-depth, cooler)
3. **Interference haze** — where layers 1 and 2 overlap (atmospheric, additive color)
4. **The hand** — point cloud (foreground, coolest, highest luminance)
