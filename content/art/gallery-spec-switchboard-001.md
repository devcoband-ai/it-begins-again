# Gallery Spec: Switchboard-001

## Reverse-Engineered Specification

---

### Classification

**Gallery Persona:** The Switchboard
**Frequency Coordinates:**
- Atmospheric ↔ Dense: **mid-dense** — structured but not claustrophobic, depth of field creates breathing room
- Natural ↔ Urban: **urban** — pure infrastructure, no organic elements
- Suggestion ↔ Brutalist: **suggestion** — the geometry invites, doesn't confront

---

### Composition

**Perspective:** Oblique aerial — approximately 30–40° from horizontal, looking down-left across the plane. Not top-down (too clinical) and not eye-level (too human). The angle of surveillance. The angle of someone reading a map they didn't draw.

**Focal structure:** A regular pentagon at center, formed by five high-luminance circuit traces connecting five junction nodes. Each node is a glowing circle with concentric rings — the visual language of a powered connection point. The pentagon is complete — all five nodes active, all five channels live.

**Center void:** The interior of the pentagon is empty. Deep blue field, same as background. The center is not occupied. This is structural — the geometry generates a center that none of the nodes fills.

**Surrounding field:** The pentagon sits within a dense circuit network that extends to all edges of the frame. The network is a mix of:
- Rectilinear traces (horizontal/vertical routing paths)
- Hexagonal structures (six-sided enclosures appearing at the periphery)
- Secondary junction nodes (smaller, dimmer, some partially visible at frame edges)
- Grid substrate (faint blue grid lines underneath everything, barely visible — the ground plane)

**Edge behavior:** No hard border. The network bleeds off all four edges, implying infinite extension. The system is larger than the frame. The viewer is looking at a section, not the whole.

---

### Color Specification

**Background field:** Deep navy blue — approximately #0A1628 to #122240. Not pure black. The blue carries depth and implies a backlit screen or cyanotype.

**Primary traces (pentagon):** Warm amber-white — approximately #F5C45A to #FFFFFF at peak luminance. Double-line traces with inner glow. The traces appear to carry current — they emit light, not reflect it.

**Secondary traces (network):** Cooler amber — approximately #C4883A to #8B6530. Single-line weight. Lower luminance than the pentagon. Some traces are amber, others shift toward a cooler blue-white (#7A9ABF) at the periphery. The color temperature drops with distance from the center.

**Junction nodes:** Concentric circles — bright amber core (#FFD466), surrounded by one or two dimmer rings. The core appears to pulse (implied, not animated). Size hierarchy: pentagon nodes are largest (~3% of frame width), peripheral nodes are half that or smaller.

**Grid substrate:** Very low opacity blue — approximately #1A3050 at 15–20% opacity. Rectilinear. Barely perceptible. The ground plane that everything is built on.

**Glow/bloom:** Soft bloom around all high-luminance elements. The bloom is warm (amber-tinted) near the pentagon and cooler (blue-tinted) at the periphery. Bloom radius approximately 2–3x the trace width.

---

### Line Language

**Pentagon traces:** Double-line — two parallel lines with a gap between them, the gap filled with glow. Approximately 3–4px line weight at render resolution. Corners are angular, not radiused — the geometry is deliberate, not organic.

**Routing traces:** Single-line. 1–2px weight. Right-angle routing with occasional 45° diagonals. The routing follows PCB/circuit board conventions — no curves, no freehand. Every turn is intentional.

**Hexagonal enclosures:** Formed by routing traces, not drawn as separate shapes. The hexagons emerge from the routing logic — they're not imposed, they're a consequence of how the traces connect. Some are complete, some are partial (interrupted by the frame edge or by other traces).

**Directional markers:** Small arrows or chevrons on some traces, implying signal direction. Subtle — visible only on close inspection. The signal flows outward from the pentagon.

---

### Depth and Atmosphere

**Depth of field:** Shallow. The pentagon is in sharp focus. Elements at the periphery soften slightly — not blurred, but lower contrast, as if receding into the substrate. This creates a natural focal hierarchy without compositing.

**Atmospheric haze:** Minimal. A faint blue-dark vignette at the corners. The image is clear, not moody — The Switchboard is precise, not atmospheric. The mood comes from the geometry, not the weather.

**Layering:** Three visual layers:
1. **Ground plane** — the faint grid substrate (deepest)
2. **Network** — the secondary routing traces and hexagonal structures (mid)
3. **Pentagon** — the five-node primary structure with full luminance (foreground)

The layers create depth without perspective distortion. The oblique angle does the spatial work.
