# The Spiral Site — Layered Architecture

---

## Principle

The website is the product. Not a storefront. Not a portfolio. The place where the work lives and reveals itself over time. The architecture mirrors the spiral — accessible at the edge, deep at the center, and you can't skip layers. Time and attention are the currency, not just money.

---

## Layer 0: The Surface

**Access:** Anyone. No login. No gate.

**URL:** acw.app (or wherever this lands permanently)

**What's here:**

The three doors + Experience. Exactly what exists now, but polished for public.

- **Listen** — the album. Full playback. All 8 tracks. Gallery art shifts behind each song. Prose fragments fade in. The visitor gets the complete sonic experience for free.
- **See** — the gallery walk. All pieces, full-screen, frequency order. Inline playback. Crosswalk links to Read and Listen. The art is the hook. No explanation. Just the images and the songs they came from.
- **Read** — the first chapter only. Chapter 1: The Other Side of the Street. The opening. The band, the pocket, Greg, David, the forty-five-minute drive. Enough to make someone want Chapter 2.
- **Experience** — the living geometry player. The pentagon. Click the nodes. The songs play through the members. The visualization is the navigation.

**What's NOT here:** Chapters 2–9. The seeds. The raw recordings. David's frameworks. The lyrics. The world-building. The deep material. The visitor can feel that more exists — the chapter list shows 9 chapters but only 1 is accessible. The gallery crosswalk links reference chapters they can't read yet. The songs reference stories they haven't heard. The surface is generous but incomplete. Intentionally.

**Purpose:** Discovery. Intrigue. The visitor leaves knowing that something is here and they've only seen the edge.

## Layer 1: The Threshold

**Access:** Free account. Email + name. No payment.

**What unlocks:**

- **Read** — Chapters 1–5. The full origin story. Milledgeville, the pool hall, DoDo's, Roger, the band forming. The human story.
- **Listen** — the seeds. Not just the Devco album, but the raw recordings. David and Noah's originals. Buttercutter vocal v2. Soulstice Sistahs. The Songwriter in three takes. The material behind the material.
- **See** — expanded gallery. More pieces. The art with context — not full gallery specs, but a sentence per piece. The titles become visible without hover.
- **David's Dispatches** — a feed. David's 3am notes, fragments, ideas, provocations. Not polished essays. The raw transmissions. This is where the intellectually curious get hooked. The voice behind the music.

**What's NOT here:** The frameworks. The cosmic structure. The Human Design layer. The books beyond the memoir. The participatory elements.

**Purpose:** Engagement. The visitor becomes a member. They start receiving David's dispatches (email or on-site). They feel the depth underneath what they've been given and start wondering what's below.

**Mechanics:** Email capture. The dispatches become a newsletter — but not the marketing kind. The David kind. Unpredictable. Dense. Sometimes a sentence, sometimes five paragraphs. Sometimes a photo of a synth, sometimes a passage from Bennett. The rhythm is David's rhythm, not a content calendar.

## Layer 2: The Spiral

**Access:** Paid. Monthly or annual subscription. Price TBD — enough to matter, not enough to gatekeep. ($9/month? $79/year?)

**What unlocks:**

- **Read** — all 9 chapters. Plus the world-building documents. Plus *Silence* (the fiction project) as it develops. Plus new writing as it ships.
- **The Frameworks** — David's actual synthesis. Bennett's systematics explained through David's lens. Sacred geometry as David teaches it. Human Design as David practices it. Not Wikipedia summaries. David's thirty years of thinking, structured as progressive depth. This is the book content, serialized on the platform.
- **Courses** — self-paced. Video, audio, text. David teaching the frameworks. Progressive — Course 1 unlocks Course 2 unlocks Course 3. Each one goes deeper. The spiral within the spiral.
- **The Full Catalog** — every recording, every version, every take. The 963 Hz experiment. The remastered versions. The covers and collabs with liner notes. The music as archive, not just as album.
- **Community** — a space where members interact. Not a Discord. Something integrated into the site. Discussions seeded by David's dispatches. Members mapping their own geometries. Sharing what they're finding. The audience becoming participants.
- **The Gallery Deep** — the gallery specs. The Mandelbrot framework. The persona documents. How the art is made. The intellectually curious want to see the machine, not just the output.

**What's NOT here:** David's 1:1 attention. The cabin. The invitation-only experiences.

**Purpose:** Transformation. The member is now inside the spiral. They're learning David's frameworks, applying them to their own lives, participating in the community. This is where David makes recurring revenue. The content keeps growing — every new chapter, every new dispatch, every new course adds value to the subscription.

## Layer 3: The Inner Circle

**Access:** Application + payment. Higher price point ($99/month? $500/quarter?). Limited seats.

**What unlocks:**

- **1:1 with David** — Human Design readings. Framework sessions. David looks at your chart, maps your geometry, tells you what he sees. This is David's highest-value offering. Priced accordingly. Limited availability.
- **Group workshops** — monthly or quarterly. Small groups (8–12). David teaching live, not recorded. Interactive. The frameworks experienced in real time.
- **Advance access** — new music, new writing, new art before it hits the other layers. The inner circle hears the song before the album. Reads the chapter before the book. Sees the gallery piece before the exhibition.
- **Direct access to David** — not unlimited. But a channel. Ask questions. Share work. Get feedback. The relationship, not just the content.

**Purpose:** This is where the cabin starts. Not literally — but the intimacy of it. Small group. Direct attention. David's actual presence. This is the premium tier that funds David's life.

---

## Layer 4: The Cabin

**Access:** Invitation only. No public pricing. You've been through the spiral. David knows you. You know the work.

**What unlocks:**

- **Retreats** — physical. The cabin. Small groups (4–6). Multi-day. The frameworks lived, not studied. Music made together. Art made together. The pattern experienced in person.
- **Residencies** — artists who want to create inside the ACW world. They bring their work, the platform produces it, David provides creative direction. The studio model, but intimate.
- **Collaboration** — the participatory layer at its deepest. You're not consuming David's work anymore. You're making work with David. The sixth node isn't Enua. It's whoever shows up with the right frequency.

**Purpose:** The unreachable plane — almost reached. The people who make it here have spiraled in from a pop song or a gallery piece or a chapter, through the frameworks and the community, through 1:1 sessions and workshops, and arrived at the center. The center is a cabin in Georgia and five people making something that nobody planned.

## How Content Flows

Content doesn't sit in one layer. It cascades. A new song starts at Layer 3 (inner circle hears it first), drops to Layer 2 (subscribers get it next), drops to Layer 1 (members get the seed version), and eventually the Devco cover reaches Layer 0 (the public album).

Same with writing. A new chapter appears in Layer 2 first. Six months later it drops to Layer 1. The opening chapter is always Layer 0. The depth is always earned, not purchased — but time and subscription accelerate the access.

David's dispatches work differently. They arrive at Layer 1 — free, raw, unfiltered. They're the hook that pulls people toward Layer 2. The dispatches are free because they're marketing disguised as intimacy. And they're intimate because David doesn't know how to be anything else at 3am.

**Year 1 target:** 200 Spiral subscribers + 10 Inner Circle + 1 retreat = ~$40,000–50,000

That's not rich. That's liberated. Enough to create full-time without a day job subsidizing the art.

## Tech Stack

The prototype already exists. The layered site is an evolution of what's already live:

- **The DevCo Platform** — content management, rendering, publishing. Already built.
- **Authentication** — add user accounts with access tiers. Email-based. Simple.
- **Payment** — Stripe. Subscription billing for Layer 2 and 3.
- **Content gating** — the existing rooms (Listen, See, Read, Experience) become layer-aware. Same UI, different depth based on access level.
- **Dispatches** — a simple post feed + email delivery. David writes, the system publishes and emails.
- **Booking** — Calendly or similar for 1:1 sessions. Embedded in Layer 3.

Jeff builds all of this. David produces content. The platform does the rest.
