# David's Creative DNA

*"Apollinaire is in my genes somehow. And ee cummings."*

---

## Guillaume Apollinaire (1880–1918)
- Broke form on purpose. No punctuation in *Zone*, visual poetry in *Calligrammes* where words physically shape themselves into pictures.
- The poem should be an object, not just a container for meaning. Form and content fused.

## e.e. cummings (1894–1962)
- Dismantled syntax, capitalization, spacing. Made the reader work to reassemble meaning.
- The fragmentation wasn't chaos — deliberate encoding. You had to participate in the poem to receive it.

## The Thread
Both were doing what Norelli-Bachelet describes in a different register: **form as language**. The shape of the thing IS the meaning. Not decoration — structure.

David doesn't just want to write something *about* truth. He wants the writing itself to embody it — the way Apollinaire's calligrammes are simultaneously poem and picture, the way cummings forces you to see the word before you read the sentence.

## For Synthesis
This is a creative compass point. When David writes for the project, expect:
- Visual arrangement as meaning
- Fragmentation as invitation, not confusion
- Form that participates in its own content
- The reader must co-create the experience
