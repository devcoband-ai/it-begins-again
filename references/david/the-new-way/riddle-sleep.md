# The Mystery of Sri Aurobindo's Riddle Sleep

**Source:** Page 315

---

He conquered the Shadow. He becomes the Son through this plunging process, yet it is at once his own son and himself. The meaning of this is that the Line initiated by the 9 Seed is never broken. He consciously enters the kingdom of Pluto, and in this fully conscious state he comes face to face with the shadow of himself, the image thrown up at the original moment of Manifestation, when the Absolute plunges into the density of form. It is only the incarnation of the Transcendent Father who can redeem the world, in a joint tapasya with the Mother; he must himself descend upon Earth and reconstruct the act of creation, for by its unfoldment the Earth is saved; that is, the Earth is preserved as a home for the evolving soul.

The confrontation with the shadow of himself during his Riddle Sleep results in his regeneration, in his rise as the resurrected Son, who is himself "made new".

In his epic poem Savitri, Sri Aurobindo describes primarily the work of the Goddess, the power of the 6 and, to an extent, that of the 3; whereas in A God's Labour he writes only of his work. Yet in Savitri his part is incorporated, as are all the four powers. There is Satyavan and King Aswapathy; these two are one. There is Savitri and her mother, who are also one. Disciples know that Sri Aurobindo identified with both Aswapathy and Satyavan, but the extent to which this is true has perhaps not been fully grasped. The combination of powers belonging to the Goddess is generally unknown.

The work of Satyavan from the time he 'dies' until Savitri brings him back from the dead, is not dealt with in the poem. The story speaks solely of the work of the Goddess. Satyavan is merely described as entering a sleep-like state exactly one year after the day Savitri is told by the sage Narad that Satyavan is fated to die. When these twelve months are completed, he 'dies' and is described as awakening from his sleep in the last book of the poem, the twelfth. Indeed, twelve years elapsed before he did awaken from the enigmatic slumber; but this is not the subject of Savitri. This period has been a complete mystery until 1976, when he was twelve years old in his form made new.
