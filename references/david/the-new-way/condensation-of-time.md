# Form, the Condensation of Time

---

Destiny is the great enigma of the human world. No matter how acute our perceptions, how penetrating our exploratory faculties into the domain of the occult appear to be, we have not come fundamentally any closer to unravelling the human being's most tantalising mystery. Along with this question of Destiny, its companion we could say, is the enigma of Time, for if the mechanics of Destiny are not understood it is because the phenomenon of Time is the primary enigma. Destiny is a child of Time—and Time is the binding energy of Form. It is possible to say that any form, microscopic to macrocosmic, is merely Time condensed. The sport of Mother Nature in her varied and myriad display of form is actually a play of Time, a never-ending experimentation with the power of crystallisation of form. And in each form there is a destiny concealed.

In this light it is possible to understand why there are quantities of different methods of divination based upon the study of many of Nature's manifestations. During what we call primitive times the human being was especially adept at reading the message of Time in the condensation of physical form, because there was a closer contact then with the vital-emotive world, the dimension within which the primary processes of time-condensation begin to organise and reveal themselves. The so-called primitive human beings had a more intimate relationship with Nature and therefore their instincts revealed to them that in order to understand her one would simply have to look deeply at the forms of herself, in a particular manner, at a particular moment that would unveil a deeper part of her mystery. Such forms would be especially revealing if they were to be observed under carefully selected moments in the life of these forms—for example, a moment when a 'shock' was introduced which would have the power to freeze the time/space history at a determined and revelatory point. As there was the knowledge of the interrelatedness and oneness of all form, things apparently unconnected with an isolated event could be understood by a process of substitution, as it were. Thus the phenomenon of sacrificial rites was a focal point in the collective life of ancient civilisations, among other reasons for the purpose of divining the destiny of an individual, an event, a people.

## Key Concepts

- **Time creates form.** Everything we see (objects, bodies, events) is time condensed into matter.
- **Forms are frozen expressions of time.** Destiny can theoretically be read from form.
- **Ancient societies believed they could read this** through divination, rituals, and symbolic events.
- **Moments of shock or transformation** reveal deeper patterns of destiny.

**In short:** Form = frozen time, and destiny is embedded inside that frozen pattern.
