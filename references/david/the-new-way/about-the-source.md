# About the Source

## The New Way (Volumes 1 & 2)
**Author:** Patrizia Norelli-Bachelet (known as Thea)
**Subtitle:** A study in the rise and the establishment of a gnostic society

---

## What the book is

A philosophical work connected to the teachings of Sri Aurobindo and Mirra Alfassa (The Mother). The author was part of the Auroville / Integral Yoga intellectual circle and developed her own interpretation of what she called "the supramental manifestation."

Not mainstream scholarship. Sits at the intersection of spiritual philosophy, symbolic cosmology, and esoteric interpretation of Aurobindo. Some readers find it profound; others see it as speculative metaphysics.

Considered one of the more complex works in the Aurobindonian tradition because the author treats geometry, time, and evolution as a single language — chapters read like philosophy, cosmology, and symbolic mathematics simultaneously.

---

## The Central Framework

| Concept | Meaning in the book |
|---------|-------------------|
| Truth-seed (bija) | Every thing contains an inner truth essence |
| Form | The outer expression of that truth |
| Time | The process through which the seed unfolds |
| Destiny | The completed expression of the seed |

The worldview:

```
Truth Seed
    ↓
Time (evolution)
    ↓
Form
    ↓
Destiny
```

This is why the referenced chapters discuss:
- Form as condensed time
- A language of form
- Zero points and sacred space
- Cosmic cycles mapped onto Earth

---

## What the author is trying to do

Build a cosmic symbolic system that unifies:
- Sacred geometry
- Astrology
- World geography
- Vedic cosmology
- Aurobindo's evolutionary philosophy

Her thesis: Earth itself is part of a larger evolutionary design, and certain forms (cities, temples, geographic alignments, calendars) express that design. The planet acts as a time instrument within a larger cosmic evolution.

---

## The Cover Image

Symbolizes what she calls the "Zero Point":

```
Light / Truth
    ↓
Emerging through form
    ↓
Into the material world
```

The star-like structure under the light references sacred geometric foundations.
