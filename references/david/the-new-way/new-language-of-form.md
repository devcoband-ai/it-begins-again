# The New Language of Form

---

We must ask ourselves, what is it that is the common property of all peoples simultaneously upon Earth, by which they are aware that they are conscious beings and part of a consciously evolving world? Certainly this is the singular property of Time, an element that somehow or other constructs a network of collective awareness by means of which Earth societies regulate their lives and join them to the movement of the rest of our solar system and the universe beyond. For the ordinary man this link hinges only around the measure of day and night and a limited span of years; but the more evolved soul sees himself and his community inserted into a time flow of sometimes thousands of years. The Mayan civilisation, for example, was remarkable in this respect. That society was able to find its place within a system of time measurement, by means of a highly enlightened calendar and remarkable astronomical observations, which extended into hundreds of thousands of years. For the Mayan his individual status was not lessened by such an accomplishment, because he was aware of the indestructibility of the collective and individual soul and could follow their development through the aeons, through their rise as well as through times of withdrawal and recession behind the protective veils of oblivion.

The man of today is overwhelmed by such a vision and does everything in his power to limit his consciousness to a fragment of time, because his consciousness is so obscured that it can in no way extend beyond his life-span of 70 or 80 years. Society in general reflects this dread of a long term vision into the future on all levels of its existence. Each and every field of Earth life at present is characterised by a preoccupation with the finite and the temporal. There is a fear of Time at the basis of all our activities and thus all the solutions we seek for our problems only carry us further toward the establishment of a consciousness of escape which sees only the surface and finite value of all things.

India, like Mayan America, was also able to follow the development of her races thousands of years into the future. She too formulated the movement of Time based on astronomical observations in a way which reflects an enlightened understanding of humanity's purpose upon Earth. ## Key Concepts

- **Time is the shared framework of human consciousness** — the common property connecting all peoples to the cosmos.
- **Ancient civilizations (Maya, India) understood time on civilizational scales** — thousands or hundreds of thousands of years.
- **Modern people think only in individual lifetimes** (~70–80 years) — a contracted, fear-based relationship with time.
- **This short time horizon produces a "consciousness of escape"** — obsessed with the temporary, afraid of the long arc.
- **Ancient cultures used astronomy and calendars** to align human life with cosmic cycles.
- **The chapter title ("The New Language of Form")** points to geometry, time, and form becoming a symbolic language — connecting to sacred geometry and number symbolism.

**In short:** Ancient civilizations lived inside cosmic time. Modern civilization lives inside personal time.
