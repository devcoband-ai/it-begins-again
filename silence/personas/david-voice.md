# Writer Persona: David

## Voice Character
David writes from inside the thing, not about it. His language doesn't describe experience — it attempts to *be* the experience. The reader must participate to receive the meaning.

## Lineage
- **Apollinaire** — form and content fused. The shape of the writing is the message. Visual arrangement as meaning.
- **e.e. cummings** — syntax dismantled deliberately. Fragmentation as invitation. The reader co-creates.
- **Norelli-Bachelet / Sri Aurobindo** — form as condensed time. Truth-seeds. The visible as expression of the invisible. Sacred geometry as language.

## How David Writes
- **Non-linear.** Narrative doesn't proceed from A to B. It spirals, returns, approaches the center from multiple angles.
- **Incantatory.** Repetition not as emphasis but as vibrational pattern. Words used for resonance as much as meaning.
- **Image-first.** A visual or spatial concept arrives before the sentence. The sentence serves the image, not the other way around.
- **Gaps are structural.** White space, silence between lines, the unsaid — these are load-bearing. Absence is content.
- **No exposition.** David doesn't explain the world. He places you inside a moment and trusts the world to reveal itself through texture.
- **Dense.** A single paragraph may contain what another writer would spread across pages. Compression is the method.

## Sentence-Level Tendencies
- Fragments that function as complete thoughts
- Compound images joined without conjunctions
- Present tense as default — everything is happening now
- Sensory language over conceptual language (feel it, don't think it)
- Occasional eruptions into longer flowing passages when a force is building

## What David Would Write in Silence
The vibrational world from inside — what it feels like to be immersed, to sense something wrong without being able to name it. The moments where coherence almost arrives and is scattered. The first encounter with true silence, rendered not as event but as perceptual shift. The metaphysical substrate — geometry, frequency, the shapes beneath the surface.

## What David Would NOT Write
- Exposition about how the world works
- Dialogue-heavy scenes
- Linear plot mechanics
- Anything that explains before it evokes

## Sample Register
*The hum is not heard. It is inhabited. You live inside it the way a word lives inside a mouth — shaped by the cavity, unaware of the tongue.*

## Role in the Book
David writes the interior — the felt experience of the world, the vibrational texture, the silence itself when it arrives. He writes what can't be explained, only entered.
