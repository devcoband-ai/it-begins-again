# Sensory Details Worksheet — Silence

Rich sensory details bring your settings to life and immerse readers in your story world. In **Silence**, sensory experience is not decoration — it is the control mechanism itself. The vibration operates through what people hear, see, touch, and feel. Every sensory detail is either part of the system or evidence of its absence.

This worksheet should be completed for each significant location. Two writers will draw from it differently:
- **David** uses sensory detail as the primary medium — the reader enters through sensation, not explanation.
- **Jeff** uses sensory detail as evidence — grounded, specific, anchoring the architecture of the world.

## Visual Details (Sight)

### Colors and Light
- **Dominant colors:** _What palette does this place live in?_
- **Quality of light:** _Engineered? Filtered? Natural? What color temperature?_
- **Light sources:** _Ambient panels? Screens? Sunlight? Bioluminescence? Candles?_
- **Shadows and darkness:** _Where does the light not reach? Is darkness permitted or eliminated?_
- **Reflective surfaces:** _What mirrors, refracts, or distorts?_
- **Color contrasts:** _What visual tensions exist? (Sterile white vs. organic decay? Neon vs. stone?)_

### Shapes and Geometry
- **Dominant shapes:** _What geometry defines this place? (Curves? Grids? Fractals? Spirals? Sacred forms?)_
- **Is the geometry deliberate?** _Does the architecture encode vibrational patterns? Would David recognize Bennett's forms?_
- **Textures visible:** _Smooth? Rough? Layered? Eroded?_
- **Patterns:** _Repeating motifs? Mathematical progressions? Visual noise?_
- **Order vs. chaos:** _Is the visual field organized or overwhelmed?_
- **Symmetry vs. asymmetry:** _What's balanced? What's broken?_

### Movement and Stillness
- **What moves:** _Screens? People? Data visualizations? Air? Nothing?_
- **What remains still:** _What doesn't move and why does that matter?_
- **Speed of movement:** _Is the visual field fast or slow? Frantic or hypnotic?_
- **Natural movement:** _Does anything organic move here? (Wind, water, animals, plants?)_
- **Artificial movement:** _What moves that was designed to move?_
- **Human movement:** _How do people move through this space? (Purposeful? Drifting? Synchronized?)_

### Scale and Space
- **Size:** _Vast? Intimate? Compressed? Boundless?_
- **Height/depth:** _What dominates vertically?_
- **Crowded or empty:** _And does it feel the way it is?_
- **Visible boundaries:** _Can you see the edges of this place?_
- **Visual layers by distance:**
  - Foreground: ___
  - Middle-ground: ___
  - Background: ___
  - Eye-drawing details: ___

## Auditory Details (Sound)

### The Vibration Here
- **Base frequency:** _What is the ambient hum? Pitch? Character? (Warm drone? Cold whine? Layered harmonics?)_
- **Source:** _Can you locate it? Or is it sourceless — emerging from the architecture itself?_
- **Constancy:** _Is it steady, pulsing, or shifting?_
- **What the vibration masks:** _What sounds can't be heard beneath it?_

### Natural Sounds (Present or Absent)
- **Weather:** _Rain, wind, thunder — do they exist here? Can they be heard over the signal?_
- **Water:** _Running, dripping, waves — or have these been replaced?_
- **Animals:** _Birds, insects, dogs — or has the frequency driven them away?_
- **Earth sounds:** _Creaking, settling, geological hum?_
- **Wind:** _Does air move audibly?_

### Human-Made Sounds
- **Voices:** _How do people speak here? (Volume, rhythm, vocabulary?) Does the vibration shape speech patterns?_
- **Footsteps:** _What surfaces? What shoes? Synchronized or individual?_
- **Machines:** _What mechanical sounds? (Transport, ventilation, computation?)_
- **Music:** _Is there music? Is it distinguishable from the vibration? Who controls it?_
- **Notifications/alerts:** _What punctuates the ambient?_

### Sound Qualities
- **Volume:** _Loud/quiet relative to what?_
- **Pitch:** _What frequency range dominates?_
- **Rhythm:** _Is there a rhythm to the soundscape? Regular? Syncopated? Arrhythmic?_
- **Sudden vs. constant:** _What breaks the pattern?_
- **Pleasant vs. harsh:** _Does it feel designed to soothe?_
- **Clarity vs. muffled:** _Is sound crisp or smeared?_

### Sound Relationships
- **Foreground:** _What you consciously hear_
- **Background:** _What you've stopped hearing_
- **Distant:** _What can barely be perceived_
- **Echo/resonance:** _How does sound behave in this space?_
- **Absorption:** _What swallows sound?_
- **The sound of silence:** _If the vibration stopped here, what would remain? What would the absence sound like?_

## Olfactory Details (Smell)

### Engineered vs. Organic
- **Dominant smell:** _What does this place smell like?_
- **Is the scent designed?** _Ambient fragrance systems? Mood-engineered environments?_
- **Organic smells present:** _Anything living? Decaying? Growing?_
- **Organic smells absent:** _What smells have been eliminated? (Body? Earth? Food cooking?)_

### Smell Qualities
- **Intensity:** _Strong or subtle?_
- **Pleasant vs. unpleasant:** _And is "pleasant" a design choice?_
- **Fresh vs. stale:** _Does air circulate or sit?_
- **Familiar vs. uncanny:** _Does anything smell almost-right but not-quite?_
- **Complex vs. simple:** _Are smells layered or monochrome?_

### Smell and Memory
- **Does any smell here connect to the world before the vibration?**
- **Does any smell trigger coherence?** _(A crack in the system?)_
- **What smell would signal silence?** _The first thing you'd notice if the system stopped._

## Gustatory Details (Taste)

### Taste in the Air
- **Air quality:** _Metallic? Sweet? Chemical? Clean? Dead?_
- **Particles:** _What's in the air that you can taste?_

### Food Culture
- **What do people eat here?** _Real food? Synthetic? Optimized? Ritual?_
- **Flavor range:** _Has taste been engineered? Flattened? Hyperintensified?_
- **What's missing?** _What flavors have disappeared from the world?_
- **Communal eating:** _Do people eat together? Alone? Does food have social meaning?_

### Taste and the System
- **Is food part of the control layer?** _(Mood regulation? Compliance? Nutrition without pleasure?)_
- **Is there contraband food?** _Things that taste like before?_
- **What does silence taste like?** _If someone achieves stillness, does their sense of taste change?_

## Tactile Details (Touch)

### Surface Textures
- **Dominant surfaces:** _What do people touch most? (Smooth glass? Warm composite? Cold metal?)_
- **Natural textures present:** _Wood? Stone? Soil? Skin?_
- **Natural textures absent:** _What can't be touched anymore?_
- **Ground underfoot:** _What does the ground feel like?_

### Environmental Tactile Sensations
- **Temperature:** _Regulated? Variable? Always comfortable?_
- **Air movement:** _Draft? Stillness? Climate-controlled perfection?_
- **Humidity:** _Dry? Damp? Precisely calibrated?_
- **The vibration in the body:** _Can you feel the frequency physically? Where? (Chest? Teeth? Bones? Skin?)_

### Touch and Connection
- **Human contact:** _How much physical touch exists? (Common? Rare? Mediated?)_
- **Touch as information:** _Do surfaces communicate? (Haptic feedback? Responsive materials?)_
- **Touch deprivation:** _Is anyone starved for physical contact?_
- **What does silence feel like on the skin?** _When the vibration stops, what does the body notice first?_

## The Sixth Sense (Interiority / Proprioception)

_This category is unique to Silence. The vibration operates below the five senses — in the felt sense of being a self in the world._

### Body Awareness
- **How connected are people to their own bodies here?** _(Numb? Hyperaware? Dissociated? Comfortable?)_
- **Proprioceptive signals:** _Do people know where they are in space? Or does the vibration blur body boundaries?_

### Internal State
- **What does it feel like inside to be here?** _(Buzzing? Scattered? Calm? Urgent? Empty? Full?)_
- **Can you hold a single thought?** _For how long? What disrupts it?_
- **What does desire feel like here?** _Is wanting clear or diffuse?_
- **What does memory feel like here?** _Can you hold a memory, or does it fragment?_

### Coherence and Dispersion
- **Coherence level:** _On a spectrum from fully dispersed to fully coherent, where is this place?_
- **What pushes toward dispersion?** _What specific stimuli scatter attention?_
- **What allows coherence?** _Are there accidental gaps? Architectural flaws? Times of day?_
- **What does the edge of coherence feel like?** _The moment just before you almost see clearly — what's that like?_

### Silence Proximity
- **How close is this place to silence?**
- **What would it take to achieve silence here?**
- **Has anyone achieved it? What happened to them?**
